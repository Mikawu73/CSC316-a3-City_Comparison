Why This Visualization

After arriving in Toronto, I became curious about how different the city felt from my hometown, Shanghai. One of the first things I noticed was that Toronto's public transportation network seemed much smaller.

But when I searched for Toronto and Shanghai on Google Maps and inspect their datas, including area and population, it was not easy to directly see the difference. That gap between lived experience and what the map showed me is why I decided to build this visualization: to present differences across cities in a more intuitive way.

Skyline height = population. Hover on blocks for details; click city card to pin.
